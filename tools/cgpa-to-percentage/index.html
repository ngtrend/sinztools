What is CGPA?

CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average. It is a grading system used by universities to evaluate a student's overall academic performance across all semesters. CGPA is typically calculated on a scale of 0 to 10, where 10 represents the highest possible grade.

How CGPA is converted to percentage

Universities convert CGPA to percentage using specific formulas that vary by institution. These formulas are designed to provide a standardized percentage equivalent for academic purposes such as job applications, higher studies, or admissions.

Why CGPA to percentage formulas differ by university

Each university has its own grading regulations and academic policies. The conversion formulas are established by university authorities to ensure fair and consistent percentage calculations based on their specific grading scales and academic standards.
