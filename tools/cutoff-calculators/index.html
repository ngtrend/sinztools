What is an admission cutoff?

An admission cutoff is the minimum score or rank required for admission to a particular course or college. It is calculated based on your entrance exam marks, board exam marks, or a combination of both, depending on the counselling authority and admission rules.

Why cutoff rules differ by state and counselling authority

Each state and counselling authority has its own admission policies, grading systems, and reservation categories. Cutoff calculations can vary based on the board examination pattern, entrance exam weightage, course popularity, and government regulations for that particular year.
