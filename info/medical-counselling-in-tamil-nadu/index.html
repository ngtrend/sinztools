Medical Counselling in Tamil Nadu

This guide explains medical counselling in Tamil Nadu, including available courses, eligibility, admissions process, and fee concepts.

Introduction to medical counselling

Medical counselling in Tamil Nadu is the process for admitting students to medical courses in government and private colleges. It is based on NEET scores and helps allocate seats for MBBS, BDS, and other medical programs.

Medical courses available in Tamil Nadu

Common medical courses include MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery), a five-and-a-half-year program. BDS (Bachelor of Dental Surgery) is a five-year dental program. AYUSH courses like BAMS, BHMS, BUMS focus on alternative medicine. Allied medical courses include nursing and paramedical programs.

Basic eligibility for medical courses

Students need to pass Class 12 with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. They must qualify NEET for MBBS and BDS. Minimum marks and age criteria apply as per Medical Council regulations.

How medical admissions generally work

Admissions are based on NEET scores through centralized counselling conducted by the Directorate of Medical Education. Students rank colleges based on their scores. The process allocates seats in government, private, and deemed universities.

How medical college fees vary

Government colleges have lower fees due to state funding. Private colleges charge higher fees. Fees can vary by course, with MBBS generally costing more than BDS. Hostel and other costs add to the total expenses.
